## Presentation Structure

### 1. Opening Hook (30 seconds)
**Problem Statement:**
> "Everyone has a life story worth preserving, but 90% of people never capture it. Why? Writing feels intimidating. StoryWorth proved this market with a $100M+ acquisition, but they're still writing-first. Mabel is voice-first — that's the unlock."

**Your Tagline:**
> "Mabel: Your Stories, Written with Care. Journal freely. Share beautifully."

---

### 2. Live Demo (2-3 minutes)

#### Show the Flow:
1. **Dashboard** - "Here's my life story project. I'm working through modules — Childhood, School Years, Career."
2. **Module View** - "Each module has 15-20 AI-generated questions. They get smarter as I answer more — the AI learns from my previous responses."
3. **Voice Recording** - "Instead of typing, I just talk. Hit record, answer the question naturally, and Mabel transcribes it."
4. **Narrative Generation** - "Once I've answered all questions, Mabel generates a polished narrative chapter from my raw transcripts."
5. **Chapter View** - "Here's the final chapter. I can regenerate with feedback if I want changes. Once approved, I move to the next module."
6. **PDF Export** - "At any point, I can export chapters or the full book as PDF. Future: voice-cloned audiobooks and printed hardcovers."

#### Key Moments to Highlight:
- Show the **AI-generated questions** (emphasize context-learning)
- Play back a **voice recording** (demonstrate voice-first)
- Show the **before/after** (raw transcript → polished narrative)
- Display a **completed chapter** (PDF export)

---

### 3. Your Journey (1 minute)

**The Evolution:**
> "I started with Family Story Archive — helping families interview older generations. But I discovered people didn't want to be interviewers; they wanted to capture *their own* stories.
>
> So I pivoted to personal voice journaling. But free-form journaling led to abandonment — 'write your life story' felt overwhelming.
>
> The breakthrough: **module-based storytelling**. Instead of a blank page, users answer 15-20 questions per chapter. Voice-first. Mabel transforms spoken memories into polished narratives.
>
> My biggest learning? **Positioning is everything.** The technical product barely changed, but repositioning from 'memoir project' to 'journaling habit' opened up a 10x larger market."

---

### 4. Market Positioning (30 seconds)

**Competitive Advantage:**

| Feature | Mabel | StoryWorth |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Mental Model | Journaling habit | Memoir project |
| Input Method | Voice-first | Email prompts → typing |
| Pace | Journal anytime | 52 weeks, weekly deadline |
| Pricing | $4.99/month | $99/year (~$8.25/mo) |

**Your Edge:**
> "We're 50% cheaper, voice-first, and positioning as a journaling habit vs. a memoir project. That's a 10x larger addressable market."

### 5. Unit Economics (1 minute)

**AI Cost:**
> "AI cost is ~47 cents per chapter using Whisper for transcription and GPT-4o for narrative generation. At $4.99/month for 10 chapters, we maintain a 60-90% gross margin depending on usage."

**Revenue Model:**

> 1. **Subscription base** at $4.99-49.99/month
> 2. **High-margin add-ons** like audiobooks ($29.99) and hardcovers ($49.99+) — 70-80% margin
> 3. **Future Family Plan** at $19.99/month for gifting to multiple family members"

**Scale Target:**

## Q&A Preparation

### Expected Questions & Responses

#### "Why is Mabel so much cheaper than StoryWorth?"
> "We're 50% cheaper because we're voice-first and AI-powered. StoryWorth still relies on manual curation and printing. Our costs are purely AI (Whisper + GPT-4o), which scale efficiently. We make money on high-margin add-ons like audiobooks and hardcovers once users are hooked."

#### "What's your AI cost per chapter?"
> "About 47 cents per chapter using Whisper for transcription and GPT-4o for narrative generation. An 8-chapter book costs ~$3.76 in AI. At $4.99/month for 10 chapters, we maintain a 60-90% gross margin depending on usage."

#### "What if users abuse unlimited annual plan?"
> "We're betting most journalers won't exceed 10-12 chapters/month average. That's 2-3 entries per week, which is ambitious. Annual commitment also means we get cash upfront, which improves runway. If power users emerge, we can introduce a 'Pro' tier at $9.99/month for truly unlimited."

#### "How do you make money long-term?"
> "Three revenue streams: (1) Subscription base at $4.99-49.99, (2) High-margin add-ons like audiobooks ($29.99) and hardcovers ($49.99+), and (3) Future Family Plan at $19.99/month for gifting to multiple family members. Add-ons are 70-80% margin — that's where real profitability comes from."

#### "What's your biggest learning from the cohort?"
> "Positioning is everything. The technical product barely changed, but repositioning from 'memoir project' to 'journaling habit' opened up a 10x larger addressable market. That one pivot unlocked genuine product-market fit."

#### "How did you validate the problem?"
> "StoryWorth proved this market with a $100M+ acquisition. But they're still writing-first — users have to type email responses. I validated that voice-first removes the biggest barrier: blank page anxiety. People will talk when they won't write."

#### "What's your unfair advantage?"
> "I built the module-based architecture from scratch. Most competitors use free-form journaling, which leads to abandonment. My context-learning AI means each chapter gets smarter based on what you've already shared. That's proprietary tech that would take competitors months to replicate."

#### "What's your customer acquisition cost (CAC) assumption?"
> "Early stage, targeting $10-20 CAC through organic (SEO, content marketing, App Store) and paid (Meta, Google). With $4.99/month pricing and 70% annual retention, LTV is ~$42. That's a 2-3x LTV:CAC ratio, which is healthy. Once we add high-margin add-ons, LTV jumps to $60-80."

#### "What's your churn assumption?"
> "Targeting 30% annual churn, which is standard for consumer subscription apps. Key retention drivers: (1) Sunk cost — once you've recorded 5+ chapters, you're invested. (2) Habit formation — gamification (streaks, milestones) keeps users coming back. (3) Emotional lock-in — your life story is irreplaceable."

#### "How do you defend against competition?"
> "Three moats: (1) **Context-learning AI** — each chapter learns from previous ones, producing smarter questions. That's proprietary tech. (2) **Voice-first positioning** — most competitors are still writing-first. We own the 'blank page anxiety' solution. (3) **Land grab pricing** — at $4.99/month, we're capturing market share before competitors can react."

#### "What would you do differently?"
> "I'd validate pricing earlier. I spent weeks building features before testing whether users would actually pay $4.99/month. Could have saved time with landing page experiments and pre-orders."

#### "What's your go-to-market strategy?"
> "Phase 1: Organic (SEO, content marketing on 'how to journal your life story', App Store optimization). Phase 2: Paid (Meta/Instagram targeting 35-65 age group interested in journaling, memoir writing, genealogy). Phase 3: Partnerships with genealogy platforms (Ancestry.com), retirement communities, and life coaches."

## Storytelling Tips

### Do:
- ✅ **Lead with the problem** (people don't capture their life stories)
- ✅ **Show, don't tell** (live demo > slides)
- ✅ **Use concrete numbers** ("47 cents per chapter", "10,000 users = $45K MRR")
- ✅ **Acknowledge competition** (StoryWorth validates the market)
- ✅ **Share your journey** (pivots, learnings, challenges)
- ✅ **Be authentic** (this is personal — you're solving a real problem)

### Don't:
- ❌ Don't over-explain technical details (no one cares about Prisma schema)
- ❌ Don't apologize for pivots (they show you're listening to users)
- ❌ Don't say "it's like X but for Y" (define your own category)
- ❌ Don't rush the demo (let people absorb what they're seeing)
- ❌ Don't hide weaknesses (be honest about risks: power users, CAC, churn)
