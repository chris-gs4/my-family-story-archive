## Pro Tips

1. **Rehearse out loud**: Time yourself with a stopwatch. Aim for 4:30-4:45 to leave buffer.
2. **Memorize the opening**: The first 45 seconds should be smooth and confident.
3. **Have backup stories ready**: If the first recording fails, have 2-3 more childhood memories prepared.
4. **Slow down**: You'll speak faster when nervous. Breathe. Pause. Let the tech work.
5. **Smile**: This is a product born from love of storytelling. Let that warmth show.
6. **Engage the audience**: Make eye contact. Ask rhetorical questions ("Who here has tried to write their life story?").
