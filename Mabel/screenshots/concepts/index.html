Profile Screen — Design Concepts

3 competing concepts for the settings/profile screen. Ordered by rank (best = left).

Recommended #1
Concept B
Settings Card
Form fields grouped in a bordered card — clear "settings surface." "YOUR PROFILE" section label. Inputs use mabelBackgroundAlt fill for subtle contrast inside the white card. Buttons below the card: CTAButton SAVE, SecondaryButton HOME, DestructiveButton LOG OUT (burgundy). Flat white background. Circle dismiss button.
UX Science
Figure-Ground (Gestalt) + Proximity
The bordered card groups all editable fields into a clear "figure" against the white background, signaling "this is where you edit." Proximity within the card implies relatedness. Buttons outside the card create a distinct action zone. The card pattern matches ChapterReview and MyStories for cross-screen consistency.

#2
Concept A
Token Polish
Pure token swap — preserves existing layout. formLabelStyle for labels, body() for inputs, spacing tokens throughout. HOME uses SecondaryButton, LOG OUT uses DestructiveButton (burgundy replaces red). Gradient background. Lowest risk.
UX Science
Consistency (Nielsen's Heuristic #4)
Maintains the familiar form layout while standardizing all tokens. The DestructiveButton with burgundy replaces the off-palette Color.red, bringing LOG OUT into the design system. No layout disruption for existing users.

#3
Concept C
Minimal Form
Strips to essentials — no card wrapper, fields on white background. "Your Profile" as Nunito heading. Save is the only CTA; Home and Log Out are text links at the bottom. Payment shown as helper text (no disabled input). Most minimal, but may feel sparse.
UX Science
Aesthetic-Minimalist (Nielsen)
Every element competes for attention — removing the card wrapper and reducing buttons to text links minimizes visual noise. The Nunito heading adds warmth. However, text links for critical actions (Log Out) reduce discoverability per Fitts's Law, and the sparse layout may feel unfinished.
