# ChatGPT Mascot Brainstorm Prompt

> Copy everything below the line and paste it into ChatGPT (GPT-4 recommended).

---

I'm designing a mascot for an iOS app called **Mabel**. I need your help exploring distinct character directions before I commit to a visual style. Below is the full context — please read it all before responding.

## What Mabel Is

Mabel is an **AI memoir companion** — a native iOS app that helps people capture and preserve their life stories through guided voice interviews. The app interviews you chapter by chapter (like a skilled biographer in your pocket), records your spoken answers, transcribes them, and transforms them into polished written narratives. No writing required — you just talk, and Mabel writes your memoir for you.

**Tagline:** "Speak Your Memoir Into Existence."
**Emotional hook:** "Everyone's stories deserve to be heard."

## Target Users

Two equally important audiences:

1. **Storytellers** — Anyone who wants to capture their personal life story: people approaching milestones, parents wanting to leave something for their children, individuals reflecting on their experiences, or anyone who enjoys rediscovering old memories.
2. **Gift givers** — People who want to preserve a loved one's stories before they're lost. They buy Mabel as a gift for parents, grandparents, or family members.

The app must feel accessible to people of ALL ages and tech comfort levels. No writing is required — talking is the entire UX, so anyone can use it.

## Brand Personality

- **Warm, caring, approachable, trustworthy**
- **Curious and encouraging** — like chatting with a friend who genuinely wants to hear your story
- **Fun over formal** — never clinical, corporate, or techy
- **Wise but not stuffy** — knowledgeable without being condescending
- **The mascot should feel like a companion, not a tool**

## How the Mascot Appears in the App

The mascot is a central visual presence, not a background element:

| Context | Size | Role |
|---------|------|------|
| **Welcome screen hero** | ~260pt (large, dominant) | First thing users see — sets the emotional tone |
| **Screen header icon** | 28pt (tiny circular icon) | Persistent brand presence across most screens |
| **Library greeting** | ~140pt | Personalized welcome ("Good morning, Sarah!") |
| **Empty states** | ~80pt | Gentle encouragement ("No stories yet — let's start!") |
| **Small decorative** | 40–60pt | Inline moments of warmth |

**Key constraint:** The mascot must be recognizable and emotionally expressive at 28pt (a tiny circle) AND at 260pt (a large hero illustration). The design must scale gracefully.

## Color Palette Context

The app uses these colors — the mascot should feel at home within them:

- **Primary:** Teal `#2E7D6B` — CTAs, icons, active states
- **Accent:** Gold `#f9e269` — decorative highlights, mascot glow
- **Backgrounds:** White `#FFFFFF` and soft sage `#D5DFDB`
- **Text:** Dark warm brown `#2d2c2b`
- **Card borders:** Warm gray `#e8e0da`

The overall aesthetic is **clean, warm, modern** — white space is valued, warmth comes from typography and the mascot itself (not busy backgrounds).

## What I Need From You

Propose **4 distinct mascot archetype concepts** for Mabel. Each concept should be a fundamentally different creative direction — different character types, different visual styles, different emotional strategies.

For each concept, provide:

1. **Character type / species / form** — What is it? (animal, human, abstract figure, object, mythical creature, etc.)
2. **Name suggestion** — Could this character share the name "Mabel," or does a different name fit better?
3. **Personality and emotional vibe** — How does this character make users feel? What's their energy?
4. **Why it fits the Mabel brand** — Connect it specifically to memoir-telling, story preservation, the voice-first UX, and the dual audience (storytellers + gift givers)
5. **Visual style suggestion** — What art style, level of detail, and mood would best express this character? (e.g., flat illustration, textured watercolor, 3D rendered, minimal line art, etc.)
6. **Scaling strategy** — How does this character remain recognizable at 28pt (tiny icon) vs. 260pt (hero)?
7. **Potential risk or downside** — What could go wrong with this direction? Who might it alienate?

After presenting all 4 concepts:

8. **Rank them 1–4 by brand fit** with reasoning — considering the dual audience (20-somethings buying gifts AND 70-year-olds telling stories), the voice-first UX, the emotional weight of memoir preservation, and long-term brand scalability.

## Important Constraints

- The app is called "Mabel" — but the mascot does NOT have to be named Mabel.
- Do NOT default to the most "obvious" or "safe" choice. I want genuinely diverse directions.
- Each concept should appeal to the full age range (20s through 80s) — not skew too young or too old.
- The mascot must work as both a large expressive illustration AND a tiny 28pt circular icon.
- Avoid anything that could feel infantilizing, clinical, corporate, or fragile.
